About The Conference

Exploring the convergence of Computational Methods and Data Sciences.

Concept Note

The National Conference on Computational and Data Sciences in Mathematics, Statistics, and Computer Science (CSDMSC 2026) aims to provide a comprehensive academic platform for mathematicians, statisticians, computer scientists, and data science professionals to come together and share recent research developments, innovations, and best practices.

The conference focuses on the growing role of computational methods, data-driven approaches, and quantitative analysis in addressing contemporary scientific and societal challenges. Through keynote lectures, technical sessions, and interactive discussions, CSDMSC 2026 seeks to highlight current trends, emerging challenges, and future directions in mathematics, statistics, computer science, data science, and analytics.

This event also offers an excellent opportunity for young researchers, research scholars, and students to engage with eminent experts, exchange ideas, and gain exposure to interdisciplinary research that bridges theory and real-world applications.

Conference Objectives

Promote Innovation

To promote research and innovation in computational and data sciences across mathematics, statistics, and computer science.

Bridge Theory & Practice

To strengthen the integration of theoretical foundations and computational methodologies for practical problem-solving.

Interdisciplinary Collaboration

To encourage interdisciplinary collaboration among academicians, researchers, and industry professionals.

Knowledge Network

To provide a platform for knowledge dissemination, capacity building, and research networking.

Inspire Future Leaders

To inspire young researchers and students toward advanced research and innovation in quantitative and computational sciences.

Expected Outcomes

CSDMSC 2026 aims to deliver tangible value to the academic and scientific community through high-quality interactions and publications.

  • Enhanced understanding of computational and data-driven approaches in mathematics and statistics.
  • Strengthening of research culture and interdisciplinary collaboration.
  • Generation of high-quality research outputs, including conference papers and future journal publications.
  • Creation of academic and professional networks among faculty members, researchers, and industry experts.
